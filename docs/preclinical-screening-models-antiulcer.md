# Preclinical Screening Models for Antiulcer Drugs

> **Methodologies for Evaluating Efficacy and Safety of Anti-Ulcer Agents**

---

## 1. Introduction

**Objective:** To identify compounds that can either reduce aggressive factors (acid, pepsin) or enhance defensive factors (mucus, prostaglandins).

**Importance:**
- Predicts clinical efficacy.
- Determines the Mechanism of Action (MOA).
- Assesses the Therapeutic Index.

---

## 2. Classification of Screening Models

### In Vivo Models (Animal-based)
- **Acute Models:** Ethanol, NSAIDs, Stress
- **Chronic Models:** Acetic Acid
- **Antisecretory Models:** Pylorus Ligation

### In Vitro Models (Cell/Enzyme-based)
- H⁺/K⁺-ATPase inhibition
- H₂ receptor antagonism

---

## 3. Pylorus Ligation (Shay Rat Model)

**Principle:** Ligation of the pylorus leads to accumulation of gastric acid, causing auto-digestion of the mucosa.

**Procedure:**
1. Fasting of rats (24–48 hours).
2. Surgical ligation of the pyloric end under anesthesia.
3. Drug administration (IP/Oral).
4. Stomach contents collected after 4–6 hours for analysis.

**Key Parameters:**
| Parameter | Description |
|---|---|
| Gastric Volume | Total volume of gastric secretion collected |
| pH | Acidity of gastric contents |
| Free Acidity | Titratable free HCl |
| Total Acidity | Total titratable acid |

---

## 4. Ethanol-Induced Gastric Ulcer

**Focus:** Cytoprotective activity.

**Mechanism:** Ethanol causes immediate vascular stasis, mucus depletion, and oxygen free radical generation.

**Significance:** Tests if a drug can protect the mucosa independent of acid inhibition.

**Example Drugs:** Sucralfate, Misoprostol.

---

## 5. NSAID-Induced Ulcers

**Common Agents:** Aspirin, Indomethacin, Naproxen.

**Mechanism:** Inhibition of Cyclooxygenase (COX-1), leading to decreased Prostaglandin E₂ and I₂.

**Clinical Relevance:** Mimics the most common drug-induced ulcers in humans.

---

## 6. Duodenal Ulcer Models

### Cysteamine Model
- Specifically induces ulcers in the duodenum.
- Increases gastric acid secretion and delays gastric emptying.

### Histamine Model
- Commonly used in Guinea pigs.
- Stimulates H₂ receptors to cause massive hypersecretion.

---

## 7. Evaluation & Scoring

### Ulcer Index (UI)

The Ulcer Index is calculated based on the number and severity of lesions observed.

**Scoring Scale (0–3):**

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 0 | No ulcer / normal mucosa |
| 1 | Redness or superficial erosion (< 1 mm) |
| 2 | Single ulcer or erosion (1–2 mm) |
| 3 | Multiple ulcers or perforated ulcer (> 2 mm) |

**Ulcer Index Formula:**

```
UI = (Total Score of all rats) / (Number of rats)
```

**% Protection:**

```
% Protection = [(UI Control - UI Treated) / UI Control] × 100
```

### Histopathology
- Examination of epithelial integrity.
- Assessment of inflammatory markers.
- H&E staining used to visualize mucosal damage.

---

## 8. Conclusion

- Selection of the model depends on the **hypothesized drug mechanism**.
- A combination of **Shay Rat** (for secretion) and **Ethanol** (for cytoprotection) is typically used for initial screening.
- **In vitro models** are essential for high-throughput screening (HTS).
